The Alphabet

The Korean alphabet system, called Hangul (한글), has 24 letters: 14 consonants and 10 vowels.

Before anything else, let’s get some concepts down:

  1. In Korean, there are NO letter equivalents for F, R, V, or Z.
  2. As we’ll learn later on, the Q, W,X, and Y sounds can only be made with special letter combinations.
  3. “C” and “K” produce the same sound in Korean, like "kite".
  4. “R” and “L” are represented by the same letter. It’s a little complex, but as you keep learning you’ll start to understand the similarities of these sounds.

This information may be slightly overwhelming, but don’t be intimidated yet. In the menu below, you’ll explore how fun and interesting different types of Korean letters are!
