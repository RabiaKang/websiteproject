Reading and Writing: Special Vowel Combinations

In this section, we will learn some very important vowel combinations that make new sounds.

  1. (ah)ᅡ + (i)ᅵ = ㅐ “ae” as in “ay”
  2. (aw)ᅥ + (i)ᅵ = ㅔ “e” as in egg
  3. (ya)ㅑ+ (i)ᅵ = ㅒ “yae” as in “yay”
  4. (yeo) ㅕ + (i)ᅵ = ㅖ “ye” like “yes”

This may seem like a lot, but let’s discuss some patterns.

Notice how “ ㅐ” and “ㅒ” look really similar? Well that’s because they are meant to! ㅒ has one extra line in the middle, which turns its sound from “ae” to “yae”. In other words, adding an extra line as seen between the two combinations above adds a “y” sound in front of the sound.

This same pattern is seen with “ㅔ” and “ㅖ” : “e” to “ye”.
Take a few minutes to memorize these four combinations before we move on.

Next, memorize these other letter combinations. We will also throw in the silent placeholderㅇ, as it is mandatory to start a syllable with a consonant.

  1. (eu) ᅳ + (i)ᅵ = ui like “gooey”
  2. (o) ᅩ + (a) ᅡ = 와 wa like “WAtermelon”
  3. (o) ᅩ + (ae) ㅐ= 왜 wae like ”way”
  4. (u)ᅮ + (i) ᅵ = 위 wi like “wii”
  5. (u)ᅮ + (eo) ᅥ = 워wo like “woe”
  6. (u) ᅮ+ (e) ㅔ= 웨 we like “way”
  7. (o) ᅩ+ (i) ᅵ= 외 oe like “way”

외, 웨 and 왜 are actually all pronounced the same, just romanized/spelled differently! Don’t worry about romanization or spelling just yet--we will cover that later.

Take a look at these words involving special combinations:

  1. 예→ “silent placeholder ㅇ” + “ye” = “ye” (means yes)
  2. 똥배→ double consonant “ㄸ” + “oh” + ending ㅇ “-ng” = ddong. “B” + special combination “ae” = bae. Ddong bae (means “pot belly”)
  3. 의자 → “silent placeholder ㅇ”+ special combination “eui” = eui. “J’ + “ah” = ja. Euija (means chair)
