Lesson 1: Grammar 3

Let’s move onto asking simple questions! Recall that Korean is a verb-final language. This also applies for questions, because all question sentences end with the question verb-sentence structure. Compare the ending of a question versus the ending of a statement below:

Statement = -입니다 “–imnida”
Question = -입니까 “–imnikka”

As we can see, only the last syllable in the ending verb –입니다 changes when we switch it to a question. In other words, When we see –입니까? at the end of a sentence, it is always a question. With this question structure, everything before (subject and object) can be kept the same as with a statement structure!

(subject) (object)입니까? = Is (subject) (object)?

Look at the example below:

스티브는 영국 사람 입니까?= Is Steve British?

Example: 나는 마크입니다 = I am Mark.

Of course questions must be answered as well! Below is a simple chart for “yes/no”.

응 “eung” yes (casual)
아니 “ani” no (casual)
네 “ne” yes (polite)
아니요 “aniyo” no (polite)

Example Question & Answer Exchanges:

  • Q: 피터는 독일 사람입니까? Is Peter German?
  • A:네,독일 사람입니다. Yes, He is German.
  • Q:톰 씨는 교수입니까? Is (Mr.) Tom a professor?
  • A: 아니요. No

BONUS: “Which country are you from?”
어느 나라 사람입니까? = 어느(which) 나라 (country) 사람(person)입니까(question structure)?
Literally translated as: “which + country + person are?”
