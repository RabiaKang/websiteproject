CULTURE NOTE: In Korea, people don’t have to be blood-related to call each other “older brother/sister”. It is simply part of the social culture as a means to relay respect based on age rank for those slightly older than you. When referring to others with these terms, be sure to attach these terms after their names. Additionally, these terms are used in specific settings, and shouldn’t be used when meeting someone for the first time, in university or work, etc.
