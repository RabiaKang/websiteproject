Lesson 1: Grammar 2

Here we will learn about the basic topic marker in Korean sentences: 는/은 . The use of this marker is in its name. It is added to the topic of the sentence, which in the past lesson was mostly pronouns.

The two forms of Topic Markers are:
Vowel: -는
Consonant: -은

RULE: If the preceding syllable ends in a consonant, use 은 to mark the topic. If the preceding syllable ends in a vowel, use 는 to mark the topic. The topic marker 은/는 always directly follows the subject of the sentence.

    Examples:
  • 나​는 학생입니다. = I(topic)+는 am a student
  • 수잔은 남아공 사람입니다. = Susan (topic) + 은is South African.
  • 존은 캐나다 사람입니다. =John (topic) + is Canadian.

Great job, keep practicing and you’ll get the hang of topic particles soon!
