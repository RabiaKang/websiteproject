Lesson 2 Grammar 3

Here we will learn the casual form for the sentence “I have/don’t have __”.

The two verbs for this are 있다 (pronounced “it-da”) and 없다 (pronounced “up-da”) (in dictionary form). The former means “to have” while the latter means “to not have”

In casual speaking, they conjugate to:
있다→ 있어요 있다→ 있어요

*요, not formally part of the conjugation between dictionary to casual form, is added as a rising tonal intonation characteristic of casual Korean speech*

Here is how to use this structure in a sentence:
(optional subject) + [object+particle] + [ 있어요/없어요].


Examples for 있어요:

  • Q: 볼펜 있어요? Do you have a pen?
  • A: 네, 볼펜 있어요. Yes, I have a pen.
  • Q: 바나나 있어요? Do you have a banana?
  • A: 바나나 있어요 I have a banana
  • 여자 친구 있어요? Q: Do you have a girlfriend?
  • A: 네, 나는 여자 친구 있어요 I have a girlfriend.

Examples for 없어요:

  • Q: 볼펜 없어요? You don’t have a ball pen, do you?
  • A: 볼펜 없어요. I don’t have a ball pen.
  • Q: 핸드폰 있어요? Do you have a cellphone?
  • A: 아니, 없어요. No (I don’t have one)
  • Q: 돈있어요? Do you have money?
  • A: 돈없어요. I don’t have money.
